Step 1 — Check the disc is recognized:
bashdrutil status
This should show the disc, its type, and used/free space. Confirm it reports ~5 GB used on a 25 GB disc.
Step 2 — Create a small test file:
bashecho "test append" > /tmp/test_append.txt
Step 3 — Create a staging folder with your word doc (or test file):
bashmkdir /tmp/bluray_test_stage
cp /path/to/your/doc.docx /tmp/bluray_test_stage/
Step 4 — Attempt the append burn manually:
bashdrutil burn -udf -noverify -append /tmp/bluray_test_stage/
The -append flag is what the app passes for non-new discs. Watch the output carefully — drutil is fairly verbose and will tell you if it rejects the media, can't find the drive, or succeeds.
What to look for:

If it asks for confirmation or prompts, that's useful to know — the app passes no interactive flags
If it fails, the exact error message will be diagnostic
If it succeeds, check in Finder whether both the original files and the new doc are visible on the disc
